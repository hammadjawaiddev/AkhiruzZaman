Topic 03 — The Descent

Prophet Isa (A.S)

عيسى ابن مريم — Jesus Son of Mary, Peace Be Upon Him

📖

Islam honours Prophet Isa (Jesus, peace be upon him) as one of the mightiest Messengers of Allah ﷻ. He was not crucified, was raised to the heavens alive, and will return to earth before the Day of Judgement. This page covers his second coming according to Islamic teachings.

The Islamic View of Prophet Isa (A.S)

According to Islamic belief, Prophet Isa ibn Maryam (Jesus son of Mary, peace be upon him) was a noble Prophet and Messenger of Allah ﷻ. He was born miraculously without a father, performed great miracles by Allah's permission, and was raised alive to the heavens — not crucified.

وَمَا قَتَلُوهُ وَمَا صَلَبُوهُ وَلَٰكِن شُبِّهَ لَهُمْ

"And they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but it was made to appear so to them…"

Surah An-Nisa (4:157)

"…And [for] their saying, 'Indeed, we have killed the Messiah, Jesus the son of Mary, the messenger of Allah.' And they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him; but it was made to appear so to them. And indeed, those who differ over it are in doubt about it. They have no knowledge of it except the following of assumption. And they did not kill him, for certain. Rather, Allah raised him to Himself. And ever is Allah Exalted in Might and Wise."

Surah An-Nisa (4:157-158)

The Certainty of His Return

The return of Prophet Isa (A.S) is mentioned in the Quran and is described extensively in authenticated Hadith. His descent is considered among the greatest of the major signs of the End Times.

وَإِن مِّنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ إِلَّا لَيُؤْمِنَنَّ بِهِ قَبْلَ مَوْتِهِ

"And there is none from the People of the Scripture but that he will surely believe in Jesus before his death. And on the Day of Resurrection he will be against them a witness."

Surah An-Nisa (4:159)

Muslim scholars, citing this verse alongside numerous hadith, affirm that before Prophet Isa (A.S) dies — which will occur after his second coming — every person from the People of the Book will believe in him as a Prophet and Messenger of Allah, not as a deity.

His Descent — Description from Hadith

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ described in vivid detail how Prophet Isa (A.S) will descend:

"By the One in Whose hand is my soul, the son of Mary will shortly descend among you as a just judge. He will break the cross, kill the swine, abolish the jizyah, and wealth will pour forth to such an extent that no one will accept it."

Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

☁

He will descend near a white minaret east of Damascus, dressed in two garments, with his hands on the wings of two angels.

✦

When he lowers his head, droplets will fall; when he raises it, droplets like pearls will scatter.

⚔

No disbeliever will be able to withstand his breath — his breath will reach as far as his sight extends.

☪

He will find Imam Mahdi leading the Muslims in prayer and will pray behind him, honouring the ummah of Muhammad ﷺ.

His Mission After Descent

According to Islamic narrations, after his descent, Prophet Isa (A.S) will fulfill a number of important missions:

Defeating the Dajjal

The primary mission at the moment of his descent will be the defeat of Al-Masih ad-Dajjal. The Dajjal will flee when he sees Isa (A.S) — for the sight of the true Messiah will cause him to dissolve like salt in water. Isa (A.S) will pursue and kill him at the gate of Ludd.

Breaking the Cross, Killing the Swine

The hadith mentions that Isa (A.S) will "break the cross" — meaning he will clarify the truth about his own nature and mission, ending the false beliefs surrounding his previous coming. He will call all people to the pure monotheism (Tawhid) that all prophets taught.

An Era of Justice and Peace

After the defeat of the Dajjal, Isa (A.S) will rule with justice. Hadith describes a remarkable era of peace, abundance, and brotherhood among believers:

  • Enmity and hatred will vanish from people's hearts
  • Wild animals will live peacefully alongside humans
  • The earth will produce so abundantly that a group will eat from a single pomegranate and sit in its shade
  • Wealth will be distributed freely, and no one will be in need

"Isa son of Maryam will live among you for forty years (after descending), then he will die, and the Muslims will offer the funeral prayer for him."

Sunan Abu Dawud — Narrated by Abu Hurayrah (R.A)

His Physical Description

The Prophet ﷺ described the appearance of Prophet Isa (A.S). In one narration from the Night Journey (Isra wal Mi'raj), the Prophet ﷺ described seeing him:

"As for Jesus, he was a man of medium height with reddish complexion and curly hair. He was bare-chested with water trickling from his head as if he had just bathed."

Sahih Bukhari, narrated by Ibn Umar (R.A)

A Note on Shared Reverence

Muslims hold Prophet Isa (A.S) in the highest regard. It is a matter of faith in Islam to believe in him as a noble Prophet of Allah ﷻ, to love him, and to honour his name by saying "peace be upon him" (alayhis salam). The Islamic belief in his return is a matter of certainty based on Quranic and Prophetic texts.
