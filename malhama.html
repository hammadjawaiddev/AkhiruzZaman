Topic 04 — The Great War

Malhama al-Kubra

الملحمة الكبرى — The Greatest Battle of the End Times

⚔️

This page presents Islamic narrations about Malhama al-Kubra (the Great War) for educational purposes. No connection is made to any contemporary geopolitical event or conflict. All content is drawn from authenticated Hadith and is presented with an educational intent.

What is Malhama al-Kubra?

Malhama al-Kubra (الملحمة الكبرى), often translated as "The Great War" or "The Great Battle," refers to a massive, unprecedented conflict prophesied in authentic Hadith as one of the major signs preceding the appearance of the Dajjal and the descent of Prophet Isa (A.S).

The word "Malhama" in Arabic refers to a great battle with significant casualties, and "Kubra" means the greatest. This event is considered by scholars to be among the major events of the End Times sequence.

"The Hour will not come until the Romans land at al-A'maq or Dabiq. An army consisting of the best people on earth at that time will come from Madinah to face them…"

Sahih Muslim, Book of Tribulations — narrated by Abu Hurayrah (R.A)

Key Narrations About the Malhama

Several authenticated narrations shed light on the nature, context, and outcomes of this great battle. The following are among the most prominent:

"The best of the martyrs are those who fight in the front row of the Malhama; they do not turn their faces away (from the enemy) until they are killed. They dwell in the highest chambers of Paradise, and their Lord smiles at them — and when your Lord smiles at a servant, that servant is not held to account."

Musnad Ahmad — Classified as Hasan by some scholars

"The Malhama, the conquest of Constantinople, and the emergence of the Dajjal will occur within a period of seven months."

Sunan Abu Dawud — narrated by Mu'adh ibn Jabal (R.A)

The Sequence of Events

Islamic scholars, studying the various narrations, have identified a sequence of events surrounding the Malhama:

1

A truce (hudna) will be established between the Muslims and a major power. This period of peace will precede the battle.

2

The treaty will be broken by the opposing force, leading to the outbreak of the great conflict.

3

The battle will take place at locations including Dabiq and al-A'maq, regions referenced in the narrations.

4

The Muslims will emerge victorious from the Malhama, a victory that will precede other major events.

5

After the victory, some among the Muslim forces will hear news that the Dajjal has appeared among their families.

6

They will leave to face the Dajjal, but will find upon arriving that it was false rumour — then the Dajjal will truly appear.

The Treachery That Triggers the Battle

According to one narration, the peace treaty preceding the Malhama will be between the Muslims and the Romans (Byzantines, or in a broader interpretation, a major Western power). The treaty will be broken through an act of treachery:

"You will make a peace treaty with the Romans, and you and they will fight a common enemy together. Then you will be in a land of flat plains; a man from among the Romans will raise the cross, and a man from the Muslims will become angry and push him away, or kill him. Then the Romans will break the treaty…"

Sunan Abu Dawud — narrated by Dhu Mikhbar (R.A)

Scale and Nature of the Battle

The narrations suggest the Malhama will be an unprecedented conflict in scale. One narration describes that one-third of the army will be killed or flee, and one-third will be the greatest martyrs on earth, while one-third will be victorious and never be afflicted again.

The sheer scale is described in some narrations as involving seventy nations, or great numbers of fighters from both sides. Scholars caution that these numbers may be symbolic of great scale rather than precise numerical figures, as Arabic usage often employs "seventy" to indicate abundance.

The Connection to the Conquest of Constantinople

Several narrations link the Malhama to what is described as a renewed conquest or liberation of Constantinople (Istanbul). The historical conquest under Sultan Muhammad al-Fatih in 1453 CE is well established, but some scholars maintain that the narrations refer to a future event during the End Times, while others believe the historical conquest fulfills this prophecy. This remains an area of scholarly discussion.

"You have heard of a city, one side of which is on land and the other on the sea (Constantinople)? They (the Companions) said: Yes. He (the Prophet) ﷺ said: The Hour will not come until seventy thousand from the Children of Isaac attack it…"

Sahih Muslim

Lessons and Significance

The narrations about the Malhama carry important lessons for believers:

  • The importance of unity among Muslims and cooperation with those who share values of justice
  • The honour and rank of martyrdom in the path of righteousness
  • That victory ultimately belongs to those who remain firm in their faith
  • The reminder that after great trials comes relief, as the Dajjal's emergence follows the Malhama — and the Dajjal's end is certain
  • That believers should seek peace where possible but remain prepared to stand for justice
📌
Scholarly Note Muslim scholars caution against identifying the Malhama with any specific contemporary conflict. The signs of the End Times have a divine wisdom, and their recognition belongs to those who live through them, not to those who speculate from afar. Our duty is to strive for righteousness, seek knowledge, and trust in Allah's plan.
