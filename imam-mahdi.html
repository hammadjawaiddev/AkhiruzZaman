Topic 01 — End Times

Imam Mahdi

The Awaited Guide — The Rightly-Guided Leader of the End Times

ℹ️

All information on this page is presented according to Islamic teachings from authenticated Hadith sources. The name "Mahdi" (المهدي) means "the rightly guided one." No claims are made about any contemporary individual.

Who is the Imam Mahdi?

According to Islamic teachings, the Imam Mahdi is a figure whose emergence is prophesied as one of the major signs of the End Times. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ described him in multiple authenticated narrations, and scholars of Islam have studied these reports extensively.

The Mahdi is not a prophet — prophethood ended with Muhammad ﷺ — but rather a righteous Muslim leader who will establish justice across the world at a time when oppression and corruption have become widespread.

"The Mahdi is from my progeny, from the descendants of Fatimah."

Sunan Abu Dawud — Classified as Sahih by Al-Albani

Description According to Hadith

The Prophet ﷺ provided detailed descriptions of the Mahdi's physical appearance and lineage in authentic narrations:

1

He will be from the lineage of the Prophet ﷺ, a descendant of Fatimah (R.A), the daughter of the Prophet.

2

His name will be Muhammad, and his father's name will be Abdullah — matching the Prophet's own name.

3

He is described as having a broad forehead and a slightly curved (aquiline) nose.

4

He will fill the earth with justice and equity just as it had been filled with oppression and tyranny.

"The Mahdi will be from my family, of the children of Fatimah… He will be a man with a broad forehead and a slightly hooked nose. He will fill the earth with equity and justice as it was filled with oppression and tyranny, and he will rule for seven years."

Sunan Abu Dawud, Book of the Mahdi

His Emergence

According to Islamic narrations, the circumstances surrounding the Mahdi's emergence will be remarkable. He will not actively seek leadership, and it is narrated that the people will pledge allegiance (bay'ah) to him near the Kaaba in Makkah.

Some narrations describe that he will emerge after a period of great strife and conflict (fitnah) among the Muslims, and that his coming will be accompanied by distinctive heavenly signs.

The Pledge of Allegiance

According to hadith, the Mahdi will be pledged allegiance between the Rukn (corner of the Kaaba) and the Maqam Ibrahim in Makkah. He will initially be reluctant to accept this role.

يُبَايَعُ لِرَجُلٍ بَيْنَ الرُّكْنِ وَالْمَقَامِ

"A man will be given the oath of allegiance between the Corner (of the Ka'bah) and the Maqam (of Ibrahim)."

Sahih Muslim, Book of Tribulations

His Rule and Achievements

Authentic narrations indicate the Mahdi will rule for a period described variously as seven, eight, or nine years. During his rule:

  • Justice and equity will prevail over oppression
  • The Muslim ummah will be united under his leadership
  • Material wealth will be distributed abundantly among believers
  • He will lead prayers, and Prophet Isa (A.S) will pray behind him after his descent
  • Together with Isa (A.S), the forces of the Dajjal will be defeated

Imam Mahdi and Prophet Isa (A.S)

One of the most significant narrations about the Mahdi describes his encounter with Prophet Isa (peace be upon him). According to hadith, when Prophet Isa descends, he will find the Mahdi leading the Muslims in prayer. Isa (A.S) will be offered to lead, but will decline, indicating that this ummah has been honoured with the Mahdi's leadership.

"Isa son of Maryam will descend, and their leader (the Mahdi) will say to him: Come and lead us in prayer. But he will say: No, verily, among you Allah has placed leaders for others…"

Sahih Muslim, narrated by Jabir ibn Abdullah (R.A)

Scholarly Notes

The belief in the coming of the Mahdi is held by the overwhelming majority of Muslim scholars throughout history. The narrations about him are numerous and have been transmitted through multiple chains of narration (mutawatir in meaning), giving them strong evidential weight in Islamic scholarship.

Scholars caution against identifying any living individual as the Mahdi based on superficial characteristics, as this has led to numerous historical false claims. The Mahdi will be recognized by clear signs at his time.
