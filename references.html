References & Sources

Quranic verses, authenticated Hadith collections, and scholarly works

📚

All content on Akhir uz Zaman is grounded exclusively in the Quran and authenticated Hadith from recognised collections. Where hadith are mentioned, their authenticity classifications are noted. This site does not rely on weak (da'if) or fabricated (mawdu') narrations for doctrinal points.

📖 The Holy Quran

The Quran is the primary and most authoritative source in Islam. All Quranic references use the original Arabic with reference to established translations.

Surah An-Nisa (4:157–159)

On the raising of Prophet Isa (A.S) and the certainty that all People of the Book will believe in him before his death.

Surah Al-Anbiya (21:96)

On the release of Ya'juj and Ma'juj (Gog and Magog) as a sign of the Hour.

Surah An-Naml (27:82)

On the emergence of the Beast of the Earth (Dabbat al-Ard) as a major sign of the Hour.

Surah Ad-Dukhan (44:10–11)

On the great smoke (Dukhan) that will cover the earth as a major sign.

Surah Al-Hajj (22:7)

On the certainty of the Day of Resurrection and Allah's power over the dead.

Surah Az-Zumar (39:68)

On the blowing of the trumpet on the Day of Judgement by Israfil (A.S).

Surah Al-Kahf (18:1–10)

Recitation of the first ten verses is recommended by the Prophet ﷺ as protection against the Dajjal.

📕 Sahih al-Bukhari

Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Ismail al-Bukhari (810–870 CE). Considered the most authentic book after the Quran by the majority of Sunni Muslim scholars.

Book of Tribulations (Kitab al-Fitan)

Contains numerous narrations about the Dajjal, including his physical description, his abilities, and the warning that every Prophet warned his nation about him.

Book of the Prophets (Kitab al-Anbiya)

Contains the description of Prophet Isa (A.S) as seen during the Isra wal Mi'raj (Night Journey).

Hadith of Jibril — Book of Faith (Kitab al-Iman)

The famous hadith in which Angel Jibril (A.S) questions the Prophet ﷺ about Islam, Iman, and Ihsan, and asks about the Hour and its signs.

📗 Sahih Muslim

Compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj al-Qushayri al-Naysaburi (815–875 CE). The second most authentic hadith collection, paired with Sahih Bukhari as the "Two Sahihs" (Al-Sahihayn).

Book of Tribulations and Signs of the Hour (Kitab al-Fitan wa Ashrat al-Sa'ah)

The most comprehensive single source for narrations about the Dajjal, Imam Mahdi, Prophet Isa (A.S), Ya'juj Ma'juj, and the major signs. Key narrators include Abu Hurayrah, Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman, and al-Nawwas ibn Sam'an (R.A).

Hadith of al-Nawwas ibn Sam'an

One of the most detailed single narrations about the Dajjal, the descent of Isa (A.S), and the emergence of Ya'juj and Ma'juj — all in one extended narration.

Narration on the Pledge of Allegiance to the Mahdi

"A man will be given the oath of allegiance between the Corner (of the Ka'bah) and the Maqam (of Ibrahim)…" — Establishes the location of the Mahdi's bay'ah.

📘 Sunan & Musnad Collections

Sunan Abu Dawud — Book of the Mahdi (Kitab al-Mahdi)

Compiled by Imam Abu Dawud al-Sijistani (817–889 CE). Contains some of the most important narrations specifically about the Mahdi, including his lineage, description, and duration of rule.

Jami al-Tirmidhi

Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Isa al-Tirmidhi (824–892 CE). Contains the hadith: "The son of Maryam will kill the Dajjal at the gate of Ludd" — classified as Sahih.

Sunan Ibn Majah

Compiled by Imam Ibn Majah al-Qazwini (824–887 CE). Contains additional narrations about the Mahdi and the End Times signs.

Musnad Ahmad ibn Hanbal

Compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal (780–855 CE). One of the largest hadith collections, containing narrations about the Malhama al-Kubra and the virtues of martyrdom.

Sunan al-Nasa'i

Compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb al-Nasa'i (829–915 CE). Part of the Six Major Hadith Collections (Al-Kutub al-Sittah).

🏛 Classical Scholarly Works

Al-Nihayah fi al-Fitan wal-Malahim — Ibn Kathir (1301–1373 CE)

"The End: Tribulations and Battles" — One of the most comprehensive classical works on the End Times, compiling and analysing all major narrations. A foundational scholarly reference.

Sharh al-Nawawi ala Sahih Muslim — Imam al-Nawawi (1233–1277 CE)

The authoritative commentary on Sahih Muslim, explaining the meanings and implications of End Times narrations with scholarly precision.

Fath al-Bari — Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani (1372–1448 CE)

The definitive commentary on Sahih Bukhari. Contains detailed discussions of the tribulations and signs of the Hour.

Silsilat al-Ahadith al-Sahihah — Imam al-Albani (1914–1999 CE)

A modern authentication work by Sheikh Muhammad Nasiruddin al-Albani, providing grading and analysis of hadith including those on the Mahdi and End Times.

⚖️
Note on Authenticity The grading of hadith as Sahih (authentic), Hasan (good), Da'if (weak), or Mawdu' (fabricated) is a precise science in Islamic scholarship. This website only uses narrations graded as Sahih or Hasan by recognised hadith scholars. When scholars differ on a hadith's grading, this is noted. Readers are encouraged to study these primary sources directly and consult qualified Islamic scholars.
